Jupiter

Radius:

69,911 km

Distance from the Sun:

778,000,000 km

How many moons:

80-95 moons. The four largest --- Io, Europa, Ganymede, and Callisto.

Atmosphere:

The composition is mostly hydrogen and helium. The top layer of the sky is probably made of ammonia ice, while the middle layer is likely made of ammonium hydrosulfide crystals. The innermost layer may be made of water ice and vapor. The vivid colors you see in thick bands across Jupiter may be plumes of sulfur and phosphorus-containing gases rising from the planet's warmer interior.

Discovery:

Jupiter has been known of since ancient times, Galileo Galilei made detailed observations in 1610.

Fun Facts:

Fact 1: Jupiter is the biggest planet in the our solar system
Fact 2: Jupiter is named after the Roman god of thunder and sky
Fact 3: Jupiter has a gaint storm in the lower section of its atmosphere
Fact 4: Most of Jupiter's moons are named after the Roman god's love interests
